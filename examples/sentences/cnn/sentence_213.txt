The President of the Czech Republic has banned the U.S. Ambassador from Prague Castle following a public dispute over the former's decision to attend a Russian World War II parade. President Milos Zeman has been criticised by Ambassador Andrew Schapiro over his plans to attend the annual parade in Moscow, marking the Allied win  at the Western front in 1945, despite the crisis in Ukraine. In response to the critique, President Zeman said Ambassador  Schapiro is no longer welcome in the Prague Castle, the seat of presidency. Much to the chagrin of President Zeman, the country's prime minister together with Czech lawmakers are siding with the American. 'I think that the reaction of President Zeman was not adequate,' Prime Minister Bohuslav Sobotka said. 'I would naturally welcome it if the attitude of Mr. President to foreign policy in general was a bit more professional,' Sobotka told the Czech public television on Monday. 'We should be able to communicate with our friends and allies,' Sobotka added.
